POWERFUL LIFE LESSONS

Curated by @nishxmail | Follow for more powerful thoughts & reflections

1. Learn how to say 'No' and mean it.
2. Resentment is like drinking poison and then hoping it will kill your enemies.
3. Life doesn’t get easier or more forgiving—we get stronger and more resilient.
4. Time truly flies. Be grateful always because it can always be worse. Live like there’s no tomorrow, and do what you love. The money will flow.
5. Chasing the opposite sex and having temporary relationships (which mean nothing but sex) is pointless.
6. If it can be fixed, why are you complaining? If it can’t be fixed, why are you complaining?
7. If the path before you is clear, you’re probably on someone else’s.
8. A private life is a happy life. Sometimes, it feels better not to talk at all—about anything.
9. Don’t say 'yes' to everyone to make them comfortable. Check with yourself too before you commit.
10. Every person has three faces: The one we show to the world and strangers, the one we show to family and close friends, and the one we show only to ourselves.
11. There is a healthy amount of distrust to have in everyone. Nobody’s intentions are truly clear, even after being stated.
12. You aren’t crazy—you’re intelligent. Life is not a matter of holding good cards but of playing a poor hand well.
13. Thousands of candles can be lit from a single candle, and its life will not get shorter. Happiness doesn’t diminish when you share it.
14. At first, you choose a partner based on appearance and enjoy it—until you realize your children will be raised not based on appearance but on values.
15. If you expect the world to be fair with you because you’re fair, you’re fooling yourself. That’s like expecting the lion not to eat you because you didn’t eat him.
16. You can always earn money—it’s not a big deal. Your world isn’t going to end because you don’t have a fixed income. Something else will work out.
17. Your friends want you to do good—but not better than them. The rest don’t care.
18. The 'average of five' is real. If you want to know someone, look at the five people they talk to most and spend the most time with.
19. If you’re trying to break a habit, don’t say, 'This is the last time I’m doing it.' Instead, say, 'This is the first time I’m not doing it.'
